# Pulsar

Pulsar is a personal repository that brings together a small set of AI-driven projects built around one core idea: helping decision-makers move from raw information to structured judgment faster and with more clarity. Rather than presenting isolated technical experiments, this repository groups related systems under a common thesis: practical intelligence tools should reduce noise, surface signal, and make reasoning more explicit.

***

## 👤 About me

I am Emanuele Casali, an operations and technology leader with a background in large-scale execution, analytical decision-making, and business transformation.
This repository is not meant to be a collection of generic AI demos. It is a compact portfolio of weekend projects that explore how structured AI workflows can support research, briefing, and decision-making in information-heavy environments.

***

## 🎯 Aim of this repository

The aim of Pulsar is to document and showcase practical systems for:

- turning fragmented information into usable insight,
- creating repeatable decision-support workflows,
- testing how AI can improve research quality and information compression,
- presenting that work in a professional and inspectable format.

In simple terms, this repository is the home for a small family of projects focused on **decision intelligence**.

## 🧭 Projects

### 📈 WARREN

**WARREN** is an AI-assisted equity research workflow.

Its goal is to support the early stages of investment analysis by combining financial data extraction, structured research synthesis, benchmark context, valuation framing, and final critical review into one workflow.

The system is designed around a disciplined analyst mindset rather than a generic summarization approach. Instead of only collecting data, it tries to organize the work the way an experienced investor or research analyst would: separating business quality, benchmark interpretation, valuation logic, scenario analysis, risks, and confidence.

#### Aim of WARREN

The aim of WARREN is to help answer a practical question:

**How can an AI-assisted workflow accelerate first-pass equity research without hiding uncertainty?**

It is built to explore:

- analyst-style research packets from raw ticker inputs,
- benchmark-aware interpretation,
- scenario-based valuation logic,
- explicit caveats and confidence scoring,
- clearer investment memos with better structure.

#### What WARREN tries to do

- Pull relevant market and company information.
- Organize the information into an analyst-ready packet.
- Add current context where useful.
- Produce a short research memo with judgment, not just summary.
- Make limitations visible instead of pretending to be certain.

***

### 🌍 CHARLIE

**CHARLIE** is a morning executive-intelligence workflow.

Its purpose is to transform a broad stream of current information into a concise daily briefing across macro trends, geopolitics, and strategic thinking.

The project is based on a simple observation: many professionals spend too much time scanning fragmented updates and too little time working with structured signal. CHARLIE is an experiment in compressing that noise into a lightweight briefing format that is easier to review and act on.

#### Aim of CHARLIE

The aim of CHARLIE is to help answer another practical question:

**How can AI reduce the time required to stay informed without collapsing into shallow news summarization?**

It is built to explore:

- recent, time-bounded web research,
- concise executive-style synthesis,
- topic-based briefing design,
- relevance over volume,
- lightweight delivery through a simple end format.

#### What CHARLIE tries to do

- Monitor a set of important themes.
- Search for recent developments.
- Distill them into a compact daily brief.
- Highlight implications instead of just headlines.
- Deliver a format that is easy to consume quickly.

## ⚙️ Philosophy

A few principles guide the work collected here:

- **Practical over flashy** — the focus is on usefulness, not AI theater.
- **Structured over vague** — better prompts and outputs come from clear frameworks.
- **Signal over noise** — compression matters when decision-makers are overloaded.
- **Judgment over automation** — these systems are intended to support human reasoning, not replace it.

***

## 🚧 Current state

These projects should be understood as working prototypes and research workflows, not finished production products.

Some components still rely on notebooks, third-party APIs, and private credentials. For that reason, the best way to understand the work today is through the repository structure, documentation, and sample outputs rather than through open public deployment.

***

## 📌 Why this repository exists

Pulsar exists to make the work legible.

Instead of leaving these projects as private notebooks or disconnected experiments, the goal is to document them clearly, show the thinking behind them, and create a more coherent body of work around AI-assisted decision systems.

If these projects evolve further, the next steps will likely focus on cleaner packaging, stronger observability, and more robust interfaces.
